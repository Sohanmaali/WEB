History of ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was founded in 1969 with the vision of harnessing space technology for national development. It has since played a crucial role in advancing India's capabilities in space exploration and satellite technology.

Achievements of ISRO

  • Successful Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan)
  • Chandrayaan-1 mission to the Moon
  • Navigation with Indian Constellation (NavIC)
  • Launch of numerous satellites for communication, Earth observation, and scientific research

Notable ISRO Missions

ISRO has conducted numerous missions in collaboration with other space agencies. Some of the notable missions include:

  • Chandrayaan-2 mission to the Moon
  • Astrosat - India's first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory
  • GSAT series of communication satellites

Future Plans

ISRO continues to work on exciting future projects, including:

  • Aditya-L1 mission to study the Sun
  • Human Spaceflight program (Gaganyaan)
  • Further exploration of Mars and other celestial bodies
